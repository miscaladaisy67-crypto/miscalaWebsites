Lesson 2: Computing and Other Sciences

1. Integration

This section explains the synergy between technology and science.

  • Computing: Defined as using technology to process input and produce desired output applicable to any industry.
  • Science: A system of knowledge concerned with the physical world through unbiased observations and systematic experimentation.
  • The Synergy: When combined, they create powerful applications across various research fields and industries, enhancing data collection and problem-solving.
2. Formality

Computing is classified as a formal science, an area that uses mathematically definable logic systems to generate results.

  • Examples: Mathematics, logic, computer science, statistics, cryptography, information technology, and robotics.
  • Technological Benefits: Calculators for complex math, spreadsheet software (Excel) for formulas, and AI that combines math with logic.
  • Virtual Testing: Used for testing mathematical and logical implementations in a digital environment.
3. Nature

Focuses on how computing serves natural science, which predicts natural phenomena through observation.

  • Examples: Biology, chemistry, physics, astronomy, oceanography, and environmental science.
  • Digital Applications: Electron microscopes for examining viral loads, CT scans for medical imaging, and Gamma ray telescopes for space observation.
  • Real-time Monitoring: Digital sensors deployed for monitoring oceanic and environmental changes.
4. Society

This covers the role of computing in social science, the study of society and human relationships.

  • Examples: Anthropology, archaeology, education, geography, psychology, and sociology.
  • Data Gathering: Online polls and digital census forms for population studies.
  • Observation & Instruction: Social networks for analyzing societal trends, and modern tools like laptops and projectors for learning.

References: COMP001 Module 02: Computing and Other Sciences; Formal, Natural, and Social Science Research Applications.
